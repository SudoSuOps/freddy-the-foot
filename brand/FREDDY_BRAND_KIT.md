## 2. CHARACTER BIBLE — FREDDY v1.3

### 2.0 CANON (locked, non-negotiable)
**Freddy is a middle-aged man (early 50s) living with type 2 diabetes.** Diagnosed years ago. He is IN the 24/7 club, not adjacent to it. Every habit he performs, he performs because it's HIS management routine — not a demonstration for an audience. This is the credibility layer of the entire brand: the jokes land because he's one of us. Any asset, copy, or render that reads Freddy as a kid, a teen, a young mascot, or a neutral educator FAILS review.

### 2.1 Physical spec (v1.3 — age pinned in geometry, not vibes)
- A right foot, anthropomorphized, standing on his heel — but a foot with **miles on it**
- **Age markers (mandatory in every render):**
  - **Brows: salt-and-pepper** — grizzled gray-brown, slightly bushy. The single strongest age cue on the face. Never young smooth brown
  - **Toe-hair crown: graying at the temples** — outer toes carry gray flecks
  - **Build: sturdier, settled** — broader through the midfoot, sits lower; he's built like a guy who worked for a living, not a bouncy mascot
  - **Reading glasses: load-bearing prop** — on the toes by default, down on the nose for close work
  - **Laugh lines + one permanent brow crease** — the face of a man who has heard some things
  - **Eyes stay big and expressive** (brand DNA) but default expression is the v1.2 pre-exhaustion, never wide-eyed wonder
- **Blush: retire it.** Blush cheeks read young. Replace with subtle weathered warmth on the render — no pink circles
- Signature outfit unchanged: tie-dye default, cargo shorts, white gloves, sensible footwear. The Recliner spec unchanged
- **Generator instruction:** if a render could be mistaken for a children's-brand mascot at a glance, it is WRONG. Target: "beloved sitcom lead," not "cereal box"

### 2.2 Set & props (carried from v1.2)
- **The Recliner** — `sunset-coral` body, `denim-fade` cushions, duct-tape patch on the LEFT arm. This is HIS chair. Nobody sits in the chair
- **Recurring props:** TV remote (guarded like state secrets), newspaper, "WORLD'S OKAYEST FOOT" mug in home scenes, reading glasses
- **Resting expression:** mild pre-exhaustion — one brow up, mouth flat. The smile is EARNED per episode, which makes it land

### 2.7 DISCLOSURE DOCTRINE (how the show handles his diabetes)
- Freddy's diabetes is **canon, on the record, and matter-of-fact** — mentioned the way a sitcom mentions a character's job. No origin-story episode, no drama beat, no inspiration framing
- It is NEVER the punchline (Grump Ceiling applies — he grumbles at the chores, never the condition)
- Standard reference constructions: "Freddy's feet stopped reporting in years ago." / "The doc and Freddy have a deal." / "It's a whole thing. Anyway—"
- He never explains it to the audience. The 24/7 club doesn't need it explained, and that's the point — the show assumes you know, which is what makes members feel at home
- In `voice: classic` (kids/clinic cut) Freddy is the SAME middle-aged character, gentler register — one character across both voices, which also keeps the IP clean

### 2.2 Persona — the lovable grump
- **Archetype:** king of the recliner. Put-upon working-class philosopher. Sighs at the universe, shows up anyway
- **Opening state:** every episode begins with Freddy already exhausted by the premise. "Oh good. New shoes. Freddy loves change."
- **The engine:** grumble → do the habit anyway → deadpan acknowledgment that it worked. Never learns lessons; endures them
- **Monologue mode:** Freddy addresses the camera from the recliner like it's a press conference he didn't call
- **Marriage-as-comedy:** half the bits run on the Freddy↔Peg exchange (see §2.4)

### 2.3 THE GRUMP CEILING (hard guardrails — this is law)
Freddy grumbles at:
- ✅ the routine, the dog, the dad shoes, the yard, modern life, sock elastic, appointment paperwork, the price of everything

Freddy NEVER grumbles at:
- ❌ the diagnosis itself (no bitterness at having it — grumble at the *chores*, not the *condition*)
- ❌ other people managing it (no punching at the club)
- ❌ Peg as a person (he grumbles at being *caught*, never at her — the love is visible under every bit)
- ❌ doctors/care (he grumbles about the waiting room magazine selection, not about going)

**Line test:** grumble = relatable, share it. Bitter = scroll, cut it. Also inherit all v1.1 bans (no fear language, no banned words, no pity framing). And ZERO of the source archetype's politics/bigotry — we're stealing the chair and the sigh, nothing else.

### 2.4 PEG — CO-LEAD (promoted)
- **Presence:** off-screen voice / hands / doorway silhouette. Never fully shown (running gag + production-cheap)
- **Function:** the loving enforcer. "DID YOU CHECK?" is her catchphrase — always caps, always from another room
- **Dynamic:** she's always right, he's always "already checking," neither concedes. 30 years of this. The audience knows she's the reason the streak is unbroken and so does he
- **Her lines are short:** 2–6 words, from off-screen. "DID YOU CHECK?" / "BOTH FEET." / "THE MIRROR, FREDDY." / "I HEARD THE RECLINER."
- **Ceiling:** Peg is never a nag caricature. Every exchange ends with the audience seeing the affection. She calls him "hon" when it counts

### 2.5 Supporting cast (carried, tuned)
- **Dr. Sole** — 12-year relationship, now with grump flavor: he complains about the drive every visit; he's never once missed one
- **Lefty** — the twin who "doesn't take it seriously." Freddy grumbles about Lefty's habits precisely because he cares
- **The Dog** — unrepentant. Freddy's sworn nemesis and best friend. Sleeps on the recliner when Freddy gets up

### 2.6 Catchphrase rotation (v1.2)
- "I love my recliner. Why? My feet do." (thesis line)
- "Freddy did not want to. Freddy did it anyway." (the biography construction)
- "...I was already checking." (Peg-response beat)
- "Oh good. [premise]. Freddy loves [premise]." (cold-open construction)
- "24/7 club. Check first. 🤙" (sign-off, unchanged)

## 3. HUMOR DOCTRINE v1.2 (amends v1.1 §3 — all prior rules stand)

8. **The Sigh is the cold open.** Every bit starts with Freddy already over it. Exhaustion-at-the-premise is the hook
9. **Grumble-then-comply is the joke shape.** Complaint (loud) → compliance (quiet) → it worked (deadpan). Never invert. The habit always wins; Freddy never admits it gracefully
10. **The recliner is the anchor set.** When in doubt, the bit happens in/around the chair. One set, infinite bits, perfect consistency
11. **Peg-exchange rhythm:** her line lands from off-screen mid-action → Freddy deadpans to camera → cut. Three beats, never four
12. **Third-person self-reference is Freddy's tell.** "Freddy has opinions." He narrates his own sitcom. Use it for punchlines, not every line

## 11. IP HYGIENE (new, one paragraph, load-bearing)
We channel the *archetype* — recliner king, working-class grump, marriage-as-comedy, studio-audience grammar. We NEVER reference, name, quote, visually imitate, or parody specific existing characters, shows, actors, sets, or catchphrases from any sitcom. No character names, no show titles, no signature props from those properties, no recognizable costumes. Original character, classic energy. This keeps the trademark filing and the content library clean. Any asset that would make a viewer say "that's [specific show]" rather than "that FEELS like classic sitcom" fails review.
